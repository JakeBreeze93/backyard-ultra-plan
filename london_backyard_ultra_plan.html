Training Philosophy

🔄 Repetition Over Speed

You're not training to run fast. You're training to run the same easy effort 24+ times without breaking down. Most sessions are at or slower than marathon pace.

⏱️ Time on Feet > Distance

Weekly hours matter more than weekly km. A 3-hour easy run builds more relevant fitness than a fast 15km tempo. Long slow efforts build durability.

🔁 Start–Stop–Restart

The hardest part is restarting after rest. Loop sessions practise the rhythm: run, stop, eat, reset, go again. Train the transitions.

🍽️ Fuel Under Fatigue

Practise eating 200–300 cal/hr during long sessions. Your stomach needs training as much as your legs. Race day is not the time to experiment.

🌙 Night Running

Hours 14–20 will be in the dark. The overnight sim teaches you to function when tired, cold, and wanting to sleep. It's a mental crucible.

🧠 Stay Small

Never think beyond the next loop. The monotony is the challenge. Train patience by running loops, treadmill sessions, and long efforts without music.

🎯 Race Simulations

Sim 1: Overnight (Week 5)

10:00 PM → 6:00 AM (8 hours) — Sat 9 Aug

Run 6.7km loops on the hour, every hour, for 8 consecutive laps. Use a local park loop or out-and-back that's roughly the distance.

  • Practise headlamp running, night clothing, and cold management
  • Fuel every single loop — test your race-day foods
  • Practise transitions: timer, eat, change layers, sit briefly, get back up
  • No music — embrace the monotony and darkness
  • Target 45–50 min per loop with 10–15 min rest
  • Note: The mental battle between 2–5 AM is the point

Sim 2: Daytime Volume (Week 6)

7:00 AM → 7:00 PM (12 hours) — Sat 16 Aug

Run 6.7km loops on the hour for 12 consecutive laps. This is your peak training effort — a full half-race simulation of sustained daytime running.

  • Practise the start-of-race rhythm (fresh legs, building fatigue)
  • Test all food: gels, bars, sandwiches, rice, soup — rotate hourly
  • Practise gear changes, sunscreen, hat swaps, sock changes
  • Monitor how your body responds to 80km of repetitive effort
  • Keep pace honest: 45–50 min loops, no faster
  • Last 2 laps will be in fading light — test headlamp transition
  • Goal: finish feeling tired but confident you could keep going

🏁 Race Day Strategy

Pacing

  • Target: 45–50 min per loop (6:43–7:27/km)
  • Do NOT run faster than 45 min in the first 12 laps
  • Let pace drift to 50 min in hours 18–24 — still well within the hour
  • Walk uphills from lap 1 — save running muscles for flat/downhill
  • Same pace, same effort, every single loop

Transitions (10–15 min window)

  • Arrive → Sit (30 sec only) → Eat → Drink → Assess feet/kit
  • Change socks every 6–8 hours (carry 3 pairs pre-staged)
  • Layer changes at nightfall and dawn
  • Keep area tidy — chaos costs time and mental energy
  • Set phone timer for 3 min before each hour to get moving

Night Strategy (Laps 14–20 approx)

  • Headlamp: fully charged + spare. Red-light mode preserves night vision
  • Extra layer: long sleeve + gilet at minimum
  • Warm food: soup, noodles, hot chocolate during night transitions
  • Caffeine: deploy from 2AM onwards (max 200mg every 3 hours)
  • Accept that 2–5 AM will be hard. It passes.

Mental Framework

  • Only ever think about THIS loop. Never count what's ahead.
  • Break into blocks: "Get to 12" → "Get to sunrise" → "Keep going"
  • Low moments always pass — never quit during a low
  • If doubting, commit to just starting the next loop
  • Stay emotionally neutral — highs and lows both drain energy

  • Cooler box with pre-portioned food bags (one per lap)
  • Thermos × 2 (hot water for soup/noodles)
  • Caffeine gels / pills (deploy from 2AM)
  • Electrolyte mix + water bottles
  • Cola (flat — late hours emergency sugar)
  • Paracetamol / ibuprofen (emergency only)

Recovery & Complementary Work

🧊 Sauna + Cold Plunge

2–3×/week on rest days or post-run. Aids recovery and builds heat/cold tolerance — both relevant for 24hr racing.

😴 Sleep

Prioritise 8+ hours in the build weeks. Bank sleep before race week. The overnight sim will disrupt one night — recover fully before the daytime sim.

🦶 Foot Care

Test socks and shoes in all sims. Apply anti-chafe liberally. Know your blister spots. Pre-tape if needed. Carry blister kit on race day.
